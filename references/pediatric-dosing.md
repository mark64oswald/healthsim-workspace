# Pediatric Dosing Reference

Reference data for age-based medication dosing in pediatric patients.

## Age Categories

| Category | Age Range | Weight Range (typical) |
|----------|-----------|------------------------|
| Neonate | 0-28 days | 2-5 kg |
| Infant | 1-12 months | 5-10 kg |
| Toddler | 1-3 years | 10-15 kg |
| Preschool | 3-5 years | 15-20 kg |
| School-age | 6-12 years | 20-40 kg |
| Adolescent | 13-17 years | 40-70 kg |

## Dosing Principles

### Weight-Based Dosing

Most pediatric medications are dosed by weight (mg/kg or mg/kg/day).

```
Dose = Weight (kg) × Dose per kg

Example: Amoxicillin 25 mg/kg/day for 15 kg child
Daily dose = 15 × 25 = 375 mg/day
```

### Body Surface Area (BSA)

Used for chemotherapy and some specialty drugs.

```
BSA (m²) = √[(Height cm × Weight kg) / 3600]

Example: 100 cm, 16 kg child
BSA = √[(100 × 16) / 3600] = 0.67 m²
```

### Maximum Doses

Pediatric doses should not exceed adult maximum doses.

## Insulin (Pediatric Type 1 Diabetes)

### Total Daily Dose (TDD)

| Phase | TDD |
|-------|-----|
| Honeymoon | 0.3-0.5 units/kg/day |
| Prepubertal | 0.5-0.75 units/kg/day |
| Pubertal | 0.75-1.0 units/kg/day |
| Post-honeymoon | 0.8-1.2 units/kg/day |

### Basal/Bolus Split

| Regimen | Basal | Bolus |
|---------|-------|-------|
| MDI | 40-50% TDD | 50-60% TDD divided |
| Pump | 40-50% TDD | Remainder as bolus |

### Correction Factor

```
Correction factor = 1800 / TDD (for rapid-acting insulin)
Example: TDD = 30 units → CF = 60 (1 unit drops BG ~60 mg/dL)
```

### Carb Ratio

```
Carb ratio = 450-500 / TDD
Example: TDD = 30 units → ICR = 15 (1 unit covers 15g carbs)
```

## Renal Dosing Adjustments

### CrCl Estimation (Schwartz Formula)

```
CrCl (mL/min/1.73m²) = k × Height (cm) / Serum Creatinine (mg/dL)

k values:
- Preterm infant: 0.33
- Term infant: 0.45
- Child 1-12 yr: 0.55
- Adolescent female: 0.55
- Adolescent male: 0.70
```

### Common Adjustments

| Drug | Normal Renal | CrCl 30-50 | CrCl 10-30 | CrCl <10 |
|------|--------------|------------|------------|----------|
| Amoxicillin | Standard | Standard | 50-75% | 25-50% |
| Ceftriaxone | Standard | Standard | Standard | Standard |
| Vancomycin | 15 mg/kg q6h | 15 mg/kg q8-12h | 15 mg/kg q24h | Per levels |

## Special Populations

### Neonatal Dosing Considerations

- Immature hepatic and renal function
- Increased volume of distribution
- Decreased protein binding
- Use gestational age and postnatal age for dosing
- Extended dosing intervals often required

### Obesity Considerations

| Drug Type | Dosing Weight |
|-----------|---------------|
| Hydrophilic (aminoglycosides) | Adjusted body weight |
| Lipophilic | Actual body weight (with max caps) |
| Loading doses | Actual body weight |
| Maintenance | Ideal or adjusted body weight |

```
Adjusted body weight = IBW + 0.4 × (Actual - IBW)
```
